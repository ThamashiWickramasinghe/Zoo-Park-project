Conservation Partners

Creating living landscapes for wildlife and people

Our conservation partners work within the land mosaic of human areas and protected natural areas to promote sustainable living for both people and animals. More and more research is showing us that wildlife needs access to the human-dominated regions surrounding protected areas in order to preserve the animals in these places and to ensure that humans benefit fully from ecological services. When humans coexist sustainably, wildlife benefits as well.

Worldwide partners and the zoo itself carry out Zoo Parc Zoo's international conservation efforts. By combining research, education, capacity building, habitat and species conservation, and local community support, these projects use a comprehensive approach to conservation.

Conservation Impact: Local and global programs

American Burying Beetle Recovery Program

The American burying beetle has drastically declined in numbers and range.The Zoo continues to monitor reintroduced beetle populations, and supplement annually with captive-raised beetles.

Eastern Box Turtle Breeding Program

Starting in 2019 a cooperative program between RIDEM Division of Fish and Wildlife and the Parc Zoo was started to house and breed eastern box turtles, a Rhode Island native species.

Operation Spadefoot Rhode Island

Introducing the Eastern spadefoot, the state's rarest toad. The loss of breeding habitat is causing populations of this species to significantly decline in Rhode Island and most of New England. However, hope exists today!

Project SELVA

Project Selva works with indigenous communities in the upper Amazon Basin of Peru to help provide a continuous revenue stream that does not involve timber harvest or oil extraction.

New England Cottontail Rabbit Repopulation Project

The rare New England cottontail is now a threatened species and is getting much-needed help. The Zoo together with regional partners formed the Captive Breeding Working Group as one potential solution for saving the New England cottontail.

Songbird Conservation

Zoo parc zoo participates the North American Songbird SAFE program. This program works to reduce threats to North American songbirds through education and conservation efforts. Check out our live bird webcam, learn about the SAFE program, and discover ways you too can help birds.

Timber Rattlesnake Recovery Program

This species, an integral part of the food web is seriously threatened in all New York.The Zoo has agreed to receive and hold Timber Rattlesnakes from endangered New York populations.

Tree Kangaroo Conservation Program

Native to the mountainous rainforests of Papua New Guinea this critically endangered species needs our help.Our Zoo has long supported wildlife and habitat conservation programs including a close partnership with the AZA Tree Kangaroo Species Survival Plan (SSP) breeding program, Tree Kangaroo SAFE Program, and the Tree Kangaroo Conservation Program.
